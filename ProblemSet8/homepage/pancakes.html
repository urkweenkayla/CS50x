hello, pancakes

It's surprisingly difficult to source compelling facts about pancakes. According to my research (google + reddit), in the US flapjacks are more or less than same as pancakes whereas in the UK flapjacks are something closer to a granola bar.

Despite the association pancakes have with breakfast, you can actually eat them at any time of day. Carpe diem.

One of the keys to making fluffy and delicious pancakes is by ensuring you don't overstir the batter, leaving it a little lumpy. I do not know why that is the case. If you know or find out, please tell me. I'm mildly curious.

A Love Story Recipe
  • 1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, mix 1 cup of all-purpose flour (can substitute for gluten-free), 1 tablespoon of sugar (optional), 2 tablespoons of baking powder, and 1/8 teaspoon of salt.
  • 2. Add Wet Ingredients: To the dry ingredients, add 1 cup of almond milk (or any plant-based milk) and 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Stir until just combined; the batter should be slightly lumpy. Do not overmix.
  • 3. Preheat a Pan: Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat. Lightly oil the surface with a bit of vegetable oil (or other oil of your choice).
  • 4. Cook Pancakes: For each pancake, pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the hot pan. Cook until bubbles appear on the surface and the edges look set, then flip and cook until golden brown on the other side.
  • 5. Serve: Serve the pancakes hot with your favorite toppings, such as maple syrup, fresh fruits, or a sprinkle of cinnamon.
